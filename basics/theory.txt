Key Characteristics of JavaScript's Dynamic Typing
No Declared Types: You do not need to explicitly specify whether a variable is a string, number, or boolean when you declare it.
Reassignable Value Types: A single variable can hold a number, and later be reassigned to hold a string or an object.
Types Belong to Values: In JavaScript, variables do not have fixed types; only the values assigned to those variables have types.

Contrast with Other Types of Systems
To understand how JavaScript operates, it helps to compare it to other typing disciplines:
Dynamic vs. Static: Unlike JavaScript, statically typed languages (such as Java, C++, or Go) check types at compile time. Variables must be strictly defined with their data types, and those types cannot change.
